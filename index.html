THE PYLON

The PUP Pylon, located at the main entrance, is one of the university's most prominent landmarks. Its three pillars symbolize the university's core pillars: Truth, Excellence, and Wisdom. It serves as a popular meeting spot and a symbol of the university's identity.

THE MURAL

This likely refers to the "PUP Legacy Wall," a large-scale mural unveiled in October 2019 for the university's 115th founding anniversary. Located on the side of the Main Academic Building, it depicts the university's history, its struggles, and its service to the nation.

VISITOR’S LOUNGE

The visitor's lounge is located near the entrance of the campus, adjacent to the Pylon. It serves as a waiting area for guests and visitors to the university, providing a comfortable space for them to relax before their appointments or tours around the campus.

OPEN COURT

The open court is a multipurpose outdoor area within the PUP campus. It is often used for various activities such as student gatherings, events, and recreational purposes. The open court provides a spacious environment for students to socialize and engage in different campus activities.

LAWN TENNIS COURT

The lawn tennis court is a sports facility within the PUP campus dedicated to tennis activities. It provides students and faculty members with a space to play and practice tennis, promoting physical fitness and sportsmanship among the university community.

BASKETBALL COURT

the basketball court is a popular sports facility within the PUP campus. It serves as a venue for basketball games, practices, and tournaments. The court is frequented by students, faculty, and staff who enjoy playing basketball as a recreational activity or as part of organized sports events.

SOUVENIR SHOP

The souvenir shop is a retail establishment located within the PUP campus that offers a variety of university-themed merchandise and memorabilia. It provides students, alumni, and visitors with an opportunity to purchase items such as clothing, accessories, stationery, and other souvenirs that showcase their pride and affiliation with the Polytechnic University of the Philippines.

MABINI SHRINE

The Mabini Shrine is the original, relocated house of Apolinario Mabini, the "Brains of the Revolution." The house was preserved and moved to the PUP campus. Adjacent to it is the Museo ni Apolinario Mabini or Mabini Museum.

MABINI MUSEUM

A modern, interactive museum built and managed by the National Historical Commission of the Philippines (NHCP). It details Mabini's life, his revolutionary works, and his political philosophy.

OBELISK

The Apolinario Mabini Obelisk is a tall monument located near the Mabini Shrine. It is inscribed with the university's moniker, "The Brains of the Nation," which was given to PUP by the National Historical Commission. The obelisk is a cenotaph, as it was the original site that held Mabini's remains before they were transferred to his hometown in Batangas.

FREEDOM PARK

Freedom Park is a significant open space within the PUP campus that serves as a venue for various activities and events. It is a place where students, faculty, and staff can gather for recreational purposes, social interactions, and campus-wide celebrations. The park provides a serene environment for relaxation and outdoor activities.

FOUNTAIN PARK

Foutain Park is a picturesque area within the PUP campus that features a beautiful fountain as its centerpiece. The park provides a tranquil and scenic environment for students, faculty, and visitors to relax and unwind. It serves as a popular spot for social gatherings, outdoor activities, and leisurely strolls amidst the soothing sound of flowing water.

INTER-FAITH CHAPEL

The Inter-faith chapel is a place of worship and spiritual reflection within the PUP campus. It serves as a sacred space where students, faculty, and staff from various religious backgrounds can come together for prayer, meditation, and religious ceremonies. The chapel promotes inclusivity and respect for diverse faiths within the university community.

MAIN ACADEMIC BUILDING

This is the largest and most iconic building on the Sta. Mesa campus, easily recognizable from the Pasig River. It houses numerous colleges, including the College of Arts and Letters (CAL), College of Accountancy and Finance (CAF), College of Political Science and Public Administration (CPSPA), and College of Social Sciences and Development (CSSD), as well as the main administrative offices like the Office of the President.

GAZEBO

Gazebos are small, open-sided structures typically found in parks or gardens. They provide a shaded area for relaxation, social gatherings, or outdoor activities. Gazebos are often used as a place to sit and enjoy the surrounding environment, offering a peaceful retreat within the campus.

GRANDSTAND

The Grandstand is located in the oval area of the PUP campus. It serves as a stage for various events, ceremonies, and sports activities. The grandstand also provides seating for spectators and participants, offering a vantage point to view the activities taking place in the oval.

OVAL

The Oval is a large open field within the PUP campus that serves as a venue for various sports activities, events, and recreational purposes. It provides students, faculty, and staff with a spacious area to engage in physical activities, outdoor games, and campus-wide celebrations.

TAHANAN NG ATLETA

The Tahanan ng Atleta is a dormitory facility within the PUP campus that provides accommodation for student-athletes. It serves as a residence for athletes who are part of the university's sports teams, offering them a convenient and supportive living environment while they pursue their academic and athletic endeavors.

GABRIELA SILANG BUILDING

The Gabriela Silang Building is a dormitory facility within the PUP campus that provides accommodation for female students. It serves as a residence for female students, offering them a safe and comfortable living environment while they pursue their academic studies.

FOOD AND NUTRITION BUILDING

The Food and Nutrition building provides essential facilities to support academic programs and research. These include standard classrooms, faculty rooms, and canteens, as well as specialized laboratories (like a Food Testing and Processing Center) and associated research centers (such as the CNSRR for food science). The food and nutrition programs are offered under the College of Science (CS). The specific programs include: Bachelor of Science in Nutrition and Dietetics (BSND), Bachelor of Science in Food Technology (BSFT)

SAMPAGUITA BUILDING

The Sampaguita Building houses several offices, including the Bonifacio V. Romero Communication Center on the 2nd floor and the ARCDO (Archival and Research/Documentation Office) on the 3rd floor. Located in the Sta. Mesa, Manila campus, it is situated next to the Charlie Building, with the Atin Ito Eatery in between them.

STUDENT CENTER

The Student Center provide services and programs for the holistic development of students, fostering their intellectual, social, and emotional well-being. It aims to develop responsible and productive citizens through support for academics, extracurricular activities, and personal growth, and by serving as a community hub for student services and activities.

CHARLIE DEL ROSARIO BUILDING

It is officially named the Charlie del Rosario Student Development Center and houses various student services, academic program offices, and student organizations. The building is a memorial to Charlie del Rosario, a former student activist and leader at the Philippine College of Commerce (PCC), which later became PUP.

LINEAR PARK

Linear Park is suitable for walking and provides a walking area with a length of about 1.1 acres. It's one of several parks along the Pasig River. The park offers a scenic view of the river and serves as a recreational area for students, faculty, and visitors to enjoy leisurely walks and outdoor activities.

LABORATORY HIGH SCHOOL BUILDING

The PUP Laboratory High School, located inside the Polytechnic University of the Philippines’ Mabini Campus in Sta. Mesa, Manila, was established in 1954 as the training ground for student teachers of the College of Education. It serves as both a regular high school and a laboratory school, emphasizing discipline, academic excellence, and nationalism. The school’s compound consists of several buildings with classrooms, laboratories, offices, a library, and a multipurpose hall, and in 2014 a proposal was made to construct a new two-wing facility to enhance its learning environment.

PRINTING PRESS BUILDING

The Printing Press Building houses the university's printing facilities, which support various academic and administrative needs. It is responsible for producing printed materials such as textbooks, research papers, official documents, and promotional materials for the university community.

PROPERTY AND SUPPLY MANAGEMENT OFFICE

The Property and Supply Management Office (PSMO) is responsible for the university's physical assets, including inventory, storage, and disposition. However, a search for a "Property and Supply Management Office" specifically at PUP yields a different office: the Intellectual Property Management Office (IPMO), which handles intellectual property from research.

WATER TOWER

The water tower is a tall structure within the PUP campus that stores and distributes water for various purposes. It serves as a vital component of the campus's water supply system, ensuring a steady and reliable source of water for academic buildings, dormitories, recreational facilities, and other campus needs.

NINOY AQUINO LEARNING RESOURCE CENTER

Ninoy Aquino Learning Resource Center (NALRC) is a facility within the PUP campus that provides students, faculty, and staff with access to various learning resources and materials. It serves as a hub for academic support, offering a wide range of resources such as books, journals, multimedia materials, and online databases to enhance the learning experience.

LAGOON COMMERCIAL SPACES

Lagoon Commercial Spaces (also referred as PUP Lagoon) are retail areas within the PUP campus that offer various goods and services to students, faculty, and staff. These commercial spaces provide convenient access to essential items such as food, beverages, stationery, and other everyday necessities, enhancing the overall campus experience.

AMPHITHEATER

The amphitheater is an outdoor venue within the PUP campus that serves as a space for performances, events, and gatherings. It provides a unique setting for cultural activities, concerts, theatrical productions, and other forms of entertainment, allowing students, faculty, and staff to showcase their talents and engage in artistic expression.

LAGOON PARK

The Lagoon Park is a scenic area within the PUP campus that features a beautiful lagoon as its centerpiece. The park provides a tranquil and picturesque environment for students, faculty, and visitors to relax and enjoy nature. It serves as a popular spot for leisurely walks, outdoor activities, and social gatherings amidst the serene ambiance of the lagoon.

NUTRITION & FOOD TECHNOLOGY RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT CENTER BUILDING

The Nutrition & Food Technology Research and Development Center (NFTRDC) Building is a facility within the PUP campus that focuses on research and development in the fields of nutrition and food technology. It serves as a hub for innovative studies, experiments, and projects aimed at advancing knowledge and practices related to food science, nutrition, and health.

HUMAN KINETICS BUILDING

The Human Kinetics Building is a facility within the PUP campus that focuses on the study and promotion of physical fitness, sports, and wellness. It serves as a hub for academic programs, research, and activities related to human movement, exercise science, and sports management.

TAHANAN NG ALUMNI

Tahanan ng Alumni is a dormitory facility within the PUP campus that provides accommodation for university alumni. It serves as a residence for former students who wish to stay connected to the university community while pursuing their personal or professional endeavors.

SWIMMING POOL

The Swimming Pool is a recreational facility within the PUP campus that provides students, faculty, and staff with a space for swimming and water-based activities. It serves as a venue for leisure, exercise, and aquatic sports, promoting physical fitness and well-being among the university community.

MULTI-PURPOSE BUILDING

The Multi-Purpose Building (Also known as the Gymnasium) is a versatile facility within the PUP campus that serves various functions and activities. It provides a flexible space for events, meetings, conferences, and other gatherings, accommodating the diverse needs of the university community.

ENGINEERING AND SCIENCE RESEARCH CENTER

The engineering and science research center is a facility within the PUP campus that focuses on research and development in the fields of engineering and science. It serves as a hub for innovative studies, experiments, and projects aimed at advancing knowledge and practices related to various engineering disciplines and scientific fields.

COMMUNICATION BUILDING

The Communication Building is a facility within the PUP campus that houses academic programs and resources related to communication studies. It serves as a hub for students, faculty, and staff involved in various communication disciplines, providing classrooms, laboratories, and other facilities to support learning and research in the field of communication.

The PUP Theater is a performing arts venue within the PUP campus that showcases various theatrical productions, performances, and cultural events. It serves as a platform for students, faculty, and guest artists to express their creativity and talent through drama, music, dance, and other forms of performing arts.

ENGINEERING AND ARCHITECTURE BUILDING

It serves as a hub for students, faculty, and staff involved in various engineering and architecture disciplines, providing classrooms, laboratories, and other facilities to support learning and research in these fields.

It provides accommodation options for students, faculty, staff, and visitors, offering a convenient and comfortable living environment with access to various facilities and services typically found in hotels.

NDC TENNIS COURT AND CLUB HOUSE

It serves as a venue for tennis activities, including recreational play, training, and tournaments, promoting physical fitness and sportsmanship among the university community.

It serves as a hub for students, faculty, and staff involved in various business disciplines, providing resources, support, and opportunities for learning and development in the field of business.
